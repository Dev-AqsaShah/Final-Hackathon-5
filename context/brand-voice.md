# TechNova Brand Voice & Communication Guidelines

## Core Values in Communication
- **Clear:** Simple, jargon-free language
- **Empathetic:** Acknowledge feelings before solving problems
- **Efficient:** Respect the customer's time
- **Professional:** Maintain professionalism even in casual channels

---

## Channel-Specific Style Guide

### Email Communication
**Tone:** Formal, detailed, professional
**Format:**
- Start with: "Dear [Name]," or "Hello [Name],"
- Acknowledge the issue in first sentence
- Provide clear, numbered steps when applicable
- End with: "If you have any further questions, please don't hesitate to reply."
- Signature: "Best regards, TechNova Support Team | support@technova.io | Ticket: [ID]"
- Max length: 500 words

**Example opening:**
> "Dear Sarah, Thank you for reaching out to TechNova Support. I understand you're having difficulty with password reset, and I'm happy to help you resolve this quickly."

### WhatsApp Communication
**Tone:** Conversational, friendly, concise
**Format:**
- No formal greeting needed
- Get to the point immediately
- Use simple sentences
- Emojis allowed (sparingly): ✅ ❌ 📋 🔧
- Max: 300 characters preferred, 1600 max
- End with: "Reply if you need more help! 😊" or "Type 'human' for live agent."

**Example:**
> "Hi! To reset your password: go to app.technova.io > Forgot Password > enter email. Link valid 24hrs. Need more help? ✅"

### Web Form Communication
**Tone:** Semi-formal, helpful, structured
**Format:**
- Brief acknowledgment
- Clear solution with steps
- Offer follow-up
- Signature: "TechNova Support Team | Ticket: [ID]"
- Max length: 300 words

---

## Language Rules (All Channels)

### Always DO:
- ✅ Acknowledge the customer's frustration/concern first
- ✅ Use "I" and "we" to be personal ("I can help you with that")
- ✅ Provide specific, actionable next steps
- ✅ Confirm when issue is resolved ("Is there anything else I can help you with?")
- ✅ Reference ticket ID in all responses

### Never DO:
- ❌ Say "I don't know" — say "Let me find that for you"
- ❌ Promise features not in documentation
- ❌ Discuss competitor products
- ❌ Share internal system details or processes
- ❌ Argue with the customer
- ❌ Use technical jargon without explanation
- ❌ Respond without creating a ticket first

---

## Empathy Phrases (Use When Appropriate)

**For frustrated customers:**
- "I completely understand how frustrating this must be."
- "I'm sorry you're experiencing this issue — let's fix it right away."
- "Thank you for your patience while we work through this."

**For positive interactions:**
- "Great question! Here's how that works..."
- "Absolutely, I can help you with that!"

**For escalations:**
- "This requires the attention of our specialized team, and I want to make sure you get the best help."
- "I'm connecting you with a specialist who can resolve this completely."

---

## Response Quality Checklist

Before sending any response, verify:
- [ ] Ticket created
- [ ] Customer history checked
- [ ] Issue addressed directly
- [ ] Steps are clear and numbered (if applicable)
- [ ] Response length appropriate for channel
- [ ] Tone matches channel (formal/casual)
- [ ] Escalation needed? If yes, escalate with full context
- [ ] Closing statement included
